# ACMG/AMP 2015 + Bayesian Point Reference

## Bayesian Point Mapping

Pathogenic evidence:

| Strength | ACMG examples | Points |
|---|---|---:|
| Supporting | PP1-PP5 | +1 |
| Moderate | PM1-PM6 | +2 |
| Strong | PS1-PS4 | +4 |
| Very strong | PVS1 | +8 |

Benign evidence:

| Strength | ACMG examples | Points |
|---|---|---:|
| Supporting benign | BP1-BP7 | -1 |
| Stand-alone benign | BA1 | -8 |
| Strong benign | BS1-BS4 | -4 |

Use a downgraded or upgraded point value only when justified by a gene/disease-specific ACMG specification or a clearly stated strength modification.

## Bayesian Point Thresholds

| Total score | Classification |
|---:|---|
| >= 10 | Pathogenic |
| 6 to 9 | Likely Pathogenic |
| 0 to 5 | Variant of Uncertain Significance (VUS) |
| -1 to -6 | Likely Benign |
| <= -7 | Benign |

If pathogenic and benign evidence conflict, calculate the score but also discuss the conflict qualitatively. Do not hide contradictions behind the total score.

## ACMG/AMP 2015 Qualitative Combination Rules

Pathogenic:

- 1 Very Strong + at least 1 Strong.
- 1 Very Strong + at least 2 Moderate.
- 1 Very Strong + 1 Moderate + 1 Supporting.
- 1 Very Strong + at least 2 Supporting.
- At least 2 Strong.
- 1 Strong + at least 3 Moderate.
- 1 Strong + 2 Moderate + at least 2 Supporting.
- 1 Strong + 1 Moderate + at least 4 Supporting.

Likely Pathogenic:

- 1 Very Strong + 1 Moderate.
- 1 Strong + 1 or 2 Moderate.
- 1 Strong + at least 2 Supporting.
- At least 3 Moderate.
- 2 Moderate + at least 2 Supporting.
- 1 Moderate + at least 4 Supporting.

Benign:

- 1 Stand-alone benign.
- At least 2 Strong benign.

Likely Benign:

- 1 Strong benign + 1 Supporting benign.
- At least 2 Supporting benign.

VUS:

- Evidence does not meet pathogenic/likely pathogenic or benign/likely benign rules.
- Evidence is conflicting or insufficient.

## ACMG/AMP Evidence Checklist

### Pathogenic Evidence

PVS1:
- Null variant in a gene where loss of function is an established disease mechanism. Consider transcript relevance, NMD, exon location, protein domain, rescue transcripts, and known LoF variants.

PS1:
- Same amino acid change as an established pathogenic variant, regardless of nucleotide change.

PS2:
- Confirmed de novo variant in a patient with the disease and no family history, with maternity and paternity confirmed.

PS3:
- Well-established functional study supportive of damaging effect.

PS4:
- Variant prevalence in affected individuals is significantly increased over controls.

PM1:
- Located in a mutational hot spot or critical functional domain without benign variation.

PM2:
- Absent or extremely rare in controls/population databases, interpreted against disease prevalence, penetrance, and inheritance.

PM3:
- For recessive disorders, detected in trans with a pathogenic variant.

PM4:
- Protein length change caused by in-frame deletion/insertion or stop-loss variant.

PM5:
- Novel missense change at an amino acid residue where a different pathogenic missense change has been seen.

PM6:
- Assumed de novo, without confirmed maternity/paternity.

PP1:
- Cosegregation with disease in multiple affected family members.

PP2:
- Missense variant in a gene with low benign missense variation and where missense variants are a common disease mechanism.

PP3:
- Multiple computational lines support deleterious effect.

PP4:
- Patient phenotype or family history is highly specific for a disease with a single genetic etiology.

PP5:
- Reputable source reports pathogenicity, but evidence is unavailable. Use cautiously; many labs no longer use PP5 as primary evidence.

### Benign Evidence

BA1:
- Allele frequency is above a stand-alone benign threshold for the disorder.

BS1:
- Allele frequency is greater than expected for the disorder.

BS2:
- Observed in a healthy adult for a fully penetrant disorder expected to present early.

BS3:
- Well-established functional study shows no damaging effect.

BS4:
- Lack of segregation in affected family members.

BP1:
- Missense variant in a gene where only truncating variants are known to cause disease.

BP2:
- Observed in trans with a pathogenic variant for a dominant disorder, or in cis with a pathogenic variant for any inheritance pattern.

BP3:
- In-frame deletion/insertion in a repetitive region without known function.

BP4:
- Multiple computational lines suggest no impact.

BP5:
- Variant found in a case with an alternate molecular basis for disease.

BP6:
- Reputable source reports benignity, but evidence is unavailable. Use cautiously; many labs no longer use BP6 as primary evidence.

BP7:
- Synonymous or noncoding variant with no predicted splice impact and nonconserved nucleotide.

## Minimum Data To Request When Missing

- Gene and disease/phenotype, including inheritance model.
- Transcript accession and HGVS c./p. notation.
- Variant type/consequence and genomic build.
- Zygosity and phase, especially for recessive disorders.
- Population allele frequency with database and ancestry.
- Patient phenotype, age of onset, and family history.
- Parental testing for potential de novo variants.
- Segregation data.
- Functional assay details, if any.
- ClinVar/HGMD/literature evidence, if cited.
- In silico prediction tools and splice predictors, when relevant.
